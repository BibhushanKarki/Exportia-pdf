Best practices

Our experience of more than a decade, working with companies in the European market, showed us that implementing these best practices enabled us to reach their first million Euros in sales faster.

Leads and customers

  • Successful exporters to Europe are very clear on their actual end-users and on the problem that they solve for these clients.
  • They have already secured sales with a number of large clients in their domestic market or overseas. They know their expectations in terms of customer service.
  • They are also really clear on the buying process of that customer and on their expectations in terms of customer profile.

Country

  • Successful exporters carefully select the countries that have the biggest potential for their products.
  • Small businesses which perform well in Europe focus on 1, or maximum 2, countries at a time. Once they have sufficient sales they start to extend to more countries.

Dashboard

  • Companies doing well in Europe have significant funding or sufficient turnover to fund their export to the European market.
  • This is critical to succeed, as initial sales may take between 6 months and a year to happen.
